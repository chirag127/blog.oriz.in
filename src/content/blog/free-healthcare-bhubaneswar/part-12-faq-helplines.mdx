# Part 12: Master FAQ, Helplines & Document Checklist

This final part is your "cheat sheet." Print it, bookmark it, or save it to your phone. It has every number and document you will ever need for healthcare in Bhubaneswar.

---

## 📋 Document Checklist (What to Carry)

Always carry these when visiting a hospital:
1.  **Aadhaar Card** (Original + Photocopy) — Required for registration everywhere.
2.  **BSKY Smart Health Card** (if you have one) — For cashless treatment at private hospitals.
3.  **NFSA / SFSS Ration Card** — To prove BSKY eligibility if your card is lost.
4.  **Previous Medical Records** — Old prescriptions, test reports, discharge summaries.
5.  **Passport-size Photographs** (2 copies) — Sometimes needed for new patient registration.
6.  **Bank Passbook / Account Details** — For JSY cash incentive (maternity cases).

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Can anyone get free treatment at Odisha government hospitals?
**Yes.** OPD consultation, medicines (Niramaya), and diagnostics (NIDAN) are free for all Odisha residents at state government hospitals, regardless of income.

### Q2: Are Central Government employees eligible for Niramaya?
**No.** Central and State government employees are excluded. They must use CGHS/State Medical Reimbursement.

### Q3: What if I don't have a BSKY card but need treatment at a private hospital?
You will have to pay out-of-pocket. However, you can still get **free treatment at any government hospital** without a BSKY card.

### Q4: Can I use BSKY at AIIMS Bhubaneswar?
AIIMS has **limited BSKY integration**. For most treatments, AIIMS follows its own subsidized pricing. For complex surgeries, check with the AIIMS administration.

### Q5: What is the difference between 108 and 102 ambulance?
*   **108:** For all medical emergencies (accident, heart attack, stroke, etc.). Free.
*   **102:** Specifically for pregnant women and sick newborns. Free.

### Q6: Where can I get free medicines outside of a hospital?
You **cannot** get free medicines outside a government hospital. Niramaya DDCs only operate inside government health facilities. The next cheapest option is a **Jan Aushadhi Kendra** (50-90% off branded prices).

### Q7: What if I am from another state (not Odisha)?
*   **AIIMS:** You can be treated (it's a central institution, open to all Indians).
*   **State Government Hospitals:** OPD is generally available, but Niramaya and NIDAN benefits may be restricted to Odisha residents. Check with the hospital.
*   **BSKY:** Only for Odisha residents.
