# Part 12: Master FAQ, Helplines & Document Checklist

This final part is your "cheat sheet." Print it, bookmark it, or save it to your phone. It has every number and document you will ever need for healthcare in Bhubaneswar.

---

## 📋 Document Checklist (What to Carry)

Always carry these when visiting a hospital:
1.  **Aadhaar Card** (Original + Photocopy) — Required for registration everywhere.
2.  **BSKY Smart Health Card** (if you have one) — For cashless treatment at private hospitals.
3.  **NFSA / SFSS Ration Card** — To prove BSKY eligibility if your card is lost.
4.  **Previous Medical Records** — Old prescriptions, test reports, discharge summaries.
5.  **Passport-size Photographs** (2 copies) — Sometimes needed for new patient registration.
6.  **Bank Passbook / Account Details** — For JSY cash incentive (maternity cases).

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Can anyone get free treatment at Odisha government hospitals?
**Yes.** OPD consultation, medicines (Niramaya), and diagnostics (NIDAN) are free for all Odisha residents at state government hospitals, regardless of income.

### Q2: Are Central Government employees eligible for Niramaya?
**No.** Central and State government employees are excluded. They must use CGHS/State Medical Reimbursement.

### Q3: What if I don't have a BSKY card but need treatment at a private hospital?
You will have to pay out-of-pocket. However, you can still get **free treatment at any government hospital** without a BSKY card.

### Q4: Can I use BSKY at AIIMS Bhubaneswar?
AIIMS has **limited BSKY integration**. For most treatments, AIIMS follows its own subsidized pricing. For complex surgeries, check with the AIIMS administration.

### Q5: What is the difference between 108 and 102 ambulance?
*   **108:** For all medical emergencies (accident, heart attack, stroke, etc.). Free.
*   **102:** Specifically for pregnant women and sick newborns. Free.

### Q6: Where can I get free medicines outside of a hospital?
You **cannot** get free medicines outside a government hospital. Niramaya DDCs only operate inside government health facilities. The next cheapest option is a **Jan Aushadhi Kendra** (50-90% off branded prices).

### Q7: What if I am from another state (not Odisha)?
*   **AIIMS:** You can be treated (it's a central institution, open to all Indians).
*   **State Government Hospitals:** OPD is generally available, but Niramaya and NIDAN benefits may be restricted to Odisha residents. Check with the hospital.
*   **BSKY:** Only for Odisha residents.

### Q8: I am a TCS employee who moved from Ghaziabad. Can I use BSKY?
**No.** BSKY requires:
1.  Permanent residency in Odisha.
2.  NFSA or SFSS ration card from Odisha.
3.  Being from an economically weaker section.

As a TCS employee, you do not meet any of these criteria. **Use your TCS group health insurance instead** (MediBuddy/Medi Assist). Most private hospitals in Bhubaneswar are empanelled under TCS's insurer.

### Q9: How do I check TCS health insurance network hospitals?
1.  Log in to **Ultimatix** (TCS employee portal).
2.  Navigate to **MediBuddy / Health Insurance Scheme (HIS)**.
3.  Use the "Network Hospital" search → Filter by "Bhubaneswar."
4.  SUM, KIMS, Kalinga, AMRI, and Care Hospital are typically in the network.

### Q10: Can I get an Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY) card?
*   Check eligibility at [beneficiary.nha.gov.in](https://beneficiary.nha.gov.in).
*   If your family was listed in the **SECC 2011** database, you are eligible **regardless of current employment**.
*   If you are **70+ years old**, you are eligible regardless of income (recent expansion).
*   If you do NOT fall under these categories, you are NOT eligible for PM-JAY.
*   **Helpline:** Call **14555** for eligibility queries.

### Q11: How can I eventually become eligible for Odisha schemes?
If you plan to live in Bhubaneswar long-term:
1.  **Get an Odisha Ration Card:** Apply at your nearest Mo Seva Kendra with proof of residence (rent agreement, electricity bill, Aadhaar with Odisha address).
2.  Once you have an NFSA ration card from Odisha and your income qualifies, you may become eligible for BSKY over time.
3.  **Voter ID:** Get an Odisha Voter ID to establish domicile. Apply online at [voterportal.eci.gov.in](https://voterportal.eci.gov.in).

### Q12: What are the best OPD timings to avoid crowds?
*   **AIIMS:** Arrive before **7:00 AM**. Book online the night before (slots open at midnight). Avoid Mondays (busiest day).
*   **Capital Hospital:** Arrive by **8:30 AM**. Afternoon sessions (2 PM - 4 PM) are usually less crowded.
*   **Best day to visit:** Wednesday or Thursday (generally quieter than Monday/Tuesday).

### Q13: Are there any charitable trusts that help with medical costs?
Yes, but **be very careful.** Some operate legitimately; others are scams.
*   **Legitimate:** PM National Relief Fund (PMNRF), CM Relief Fund (CMRF), Tata Trusts.
*   **Red Flags for Scams:** Anyone asking for upfront "processing fees," unregistered NGOs, trusts that ask for bank details before approval.
*   **How to verify:** Check if the trust is registered under the Indian Trusts Act at your local Charity Commissioner's office.

### Q14: What is the best time of year to get elective surgery?
*   **October-February** (post-monsoon, pre-summer): Hospital crowding tends to be lower.
*   **Avoid:** April-June (peak heat, higher infection risk) and July-September (monsoon, travel disruption).
*   **For government hospitals:** Surgical slots are allocated faster during off-peak months.
