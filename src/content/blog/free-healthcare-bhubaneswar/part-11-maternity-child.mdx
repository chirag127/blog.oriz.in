# Part 11: Maternity, Child Health & Women's Healthcare

Odisha has some of the best maternal and child health programs in India. If you or someone in your family is pregnant or has a sick child, here is how to access **completely free** care.

---

## 1. Free Delivery (Childbirth) — JSSK

The **Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK)** guarantees free delivery for **all pregnant women** at government health facilities.

### What is free under JSSK?
*   **Normal delivery** — Free.
*   **C-section (Caesarean)** — Free.
*   **All medicines** during pregnancy and delivery — Free.
*   **All diagnostic tests** (ultrasound, blood tests) — Free.
*   **Blood transfusion** if needed — Free.
*   **Free food** for the mother during hospital stay (up to 3 days for normal delivery, 7 days for C-section).
*   **Free transport** (102 ambulance to and from the hospital) — Free.
*   **Free treatment for the newborn** (up to 30 days after birth) — Free.

### Where to go?
*   **Capital Hospital** — Has a dedicated maternity ward.
*   **Hi-Tech Medical College** — Good obstetrics department.
*   **AIIMS Bhubaneswar** — For high-risk pregnancies.
*   **Any UCHC/UPHC** — For routine antenatal check-ups.

---

## 2. Cash Incentive for Delivery — JSY

Under the **Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY)**, pregnant women from BPL families receive a **cash incentive** for delivering at a government hospital:
*   **Rural areas:** ₹1,400 per delivery.
*   **Urban areas (Bhubaneswar):** ₹1,000 per delivery.

## 3. Child Health — RBSK

The **Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)** provides free health screening and treatment for children from birth to 18 years.

### What does RBSK cover?
*   **Health screening** at schools and Anganwadi centres.
*   **30 identified health conditions** are treated completely free, including:
    *   Congenital heart defects (surgery at AIIMS/empanelled hospitals)
    *   Cleft lip/palate repair
    *   Club foot correction
    *   Hearing impairment (free hearing aids)
    *   Vision problems (free spectacles)
    *   Childhood cancers (referral to AIIMS/cancer centres)
    *   Developmental delays

---

## 4. Immunization — Free for All Children

All government hospitals, UCHCs, and UPHCs provide **free immunization** for children under the **Universal Immunization Programme (UIP)**:
*   BCG, OPV, Pentavalent, Rotavirus, MR, JE, DPT Booster, and more.
*   **Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine (PCV)** — Now free.

## 5. Women-Specific BSKY Benefits

As covered in Part 4, women under BSKY get an **additional ₹5 Lakh** coverage (total ₹10 Lakh), making it one of the most women-friendly healthcare schemes in India.

---

## 6. Anganwadi Services (Nutrition)

For pregnant women and children under 6:
*   **Supplementary nutrition** (Take-Home Rations) from Anganwadi centres.
*   **Growth monitoring** for children.
*   **Nutrition counselling** for mothers.
*   Find your nearest Anganwadi via the **ICDS (Integrated Child Development Services)** office.
