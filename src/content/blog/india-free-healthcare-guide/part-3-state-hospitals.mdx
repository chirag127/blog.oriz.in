# Part 3: State Government Hospitals — The Backbone of Free Healthcare

While central hospitals grab headlines, **state government hospitals** treat 80%+ of India's patients. Every state runs a massive network of health facilities from village-level Sub-Centres to city-level Medical Colleges. Here's how the system works.

---

## The Hierarchy (Bottom to Top)

| Level | What It Is | What's Available | Typical Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sub-Centre** | Village-level health post | Basic first aid, ANM services, immunization | Free |
| **PHC (Primary Health Centre)** | Serves ~30,000 population | OPD, basic medicines, maternal care, lab tests | Free |
| **CHC (Community Health Centre)** | Serves ~1,20,000 population | Surgery, X-ray, lab, 30-bed inpatient, specialists | Free |
| **Sub-District Hospital** | Taluk/Tehsil level | All specialties, blood bank, ICU | Free |
| **District Hospital** | District headquarters | Super-specialty departments, advanced diagnostics, 200-500 beds | Free |
| **Government Medical College** | Major city | Teaching hospital with all departments, 500-2000 beds, advanced surgery | Free/Nominal |

## What's Free at State Hospitals?

This varies by state, but in general:
*   **OPD Consultation:** Free or ₹2-10 in most states.
*   **Emergency Care:** Free everywhere (by law, no hospital can refuse emergency treatment).
*   **Medicines:** Free in states with drug distribution schemes (Odisha Niramaya, Tamil Nadu MRF, Rajasthan Free Medicine Scheme).
*   **Diagnostics:** Free in states with diagnostic schemes (Odisha NIDAN, Rajasthan Free Diagnostic Scheme).
*   **Surgery:** Free at most government medical colleges and district hospitals.
*   **Inpatient care:** Free at general wards.
