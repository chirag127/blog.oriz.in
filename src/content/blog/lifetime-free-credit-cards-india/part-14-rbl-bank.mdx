# Part 14: RBL Bank — Grocery & Utility Focused

Welcome to Part 14. **RBL Bank** is highly aggressive in the co-branded credit card space (famously partnering with Bajaj Finserv and Zomato in the past). When it comes to Lifetime Free (LTF) cards, RBL focuses heavily on everyday household expenses: groceries, utilities, and movies.

---

## 🏦 RBL Bank's Core Policy

*   **Co-Brand Heavy:** RBL Bank issues dozens of variations of essentially the same card under different brand names.
*   **Fee Waivers vs. LTF:** RBL is notorious for charging annual fees on their core cards. However, if you apply through third-party financial aggregators (like BankBazaar, Paisabazaar, or Cred), they frequently offer these cards as unconditional LTF to acquire customers.
*   **Card Limits:** You can hold multiple RBL cards (e.g., a Bajaj RBL card and a core RBL card).

---

## 💳 The Everyday Household Cards

### 1. RBL Bank ShopRite Credit Card
If you manage the grocery shopping for your household, this is the card to get.
*   **Status:** Normally carries a ₹500 fee, but is routinely offered LTF via direct bank SMS links or third-party aggregators.
*   **Best For:** Groceries and Movies.
*   **Benefits:**
    *   **5% Value Back on Groceries:** Earn 20 Reward Points for every ₹100 spent on grocery shopping (capped at 1,000 points per month).
    *   **Movie Discounts:** 10% discount on movie tickets at BookMyShow (up to ₹100) up to 15 times a year.
    *   **Fuel:** Earn 10 Reward Points for every ₹100 spent on fuel.

### 2. RBL Bank BankBazaar SaveMax Credit Card
A co-branded card with the aggregator BankBazaar, explicitly designed to be Lifetime Free.
*   **Status:** Unconditionally Lifetime Free.
*   **Best For:** EMI conversions and grocery.
*   **Benefits:**
    *   5X Reward Points on groceries.
    *   10% cashback on Zomato and BookMyShow (up to ₹100 per month).
    *   Unique feature: Gives you accelerated rewards when you convert a large purchase into an EMI.

---

## 🎯 The TCS Employee Strategy

RBL Bank credit limits can be quite generous for IT employees. If you are a TCS employee, you should look out for the **ShopRite card** during promotional LTF periods. 

It serves a very specific, utilitarian purpose in your wallet. Use it exclusively at D-Mart, Reliance Smart, or BigBasket to max out the grocery reward points every month, then use your HDFC or ICICI cards for everything else.
