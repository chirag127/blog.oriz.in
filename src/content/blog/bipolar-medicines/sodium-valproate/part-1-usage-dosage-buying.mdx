# Sodium Valproate: Uses, Dosage, and Buying Guide in India (Part 1)

*Medical Disclaimer: This article is for informational and educational purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult your psychiatrist or physician before making any changes to your medication regimen.*

Sodium Valproate (and Valproic Acid) is a heavyweight mood stabilizer, forming the bedrock of many Bipolar Disorder treatment plans. In this first part of our comprehensive guide, we cover exactly what this medicine is, how to take it, and how to navigate the Indian pharmaceutical market to buy it.

---

## Section 1: What is this medicine & how it works

### 1. Drug Class & Category
*   **Primary Class**: Anticonvulsant / Antiepileptic Drug (AED).
*   **Psychiatric Class**: Mood Stabilizer.

### 2. Mechanism of Action (Simple Language)
In a bipolar manic episode, the brain's electrical signals are chaotic and firing too rapidly. Sodium Valproate acts as a chemical "brake." It boosts the levels of GABA (a calming chemical in the brain) and blocks the electrical channels (sodium and calcium) that cause neurons to misfire. 
*   **Medical Detail**: It inhibits GABA transaminase (increasing GABAergic inhibitory transmission), blocks voltage-gated sodium channels, and acts as a histone deacetylase (HDAC) inhibitor, causing epigenetic changes that stabilize mood long-term.

### 3. Approved Medical Uses (Indications)
*   **Bipolar Disorder**: Treatment of acute manic or mixed episodes.
*   **Epilepsy**: Monotherapy and adjunctive therapy for complex partial and absence seizures.
*   **Migraine**: Prophylaxis (prevention) of migraine headaches.

### 4. Off-Label Uses
*   **Bipolar Maintenance**: Used universally off-label as a long-term maintenance drug to prevent future manic episodes.
*   **Schizophrenia (Adjunct)**: To treat severe agitation.
*   **Borderline Personality Disorder**: To manage impulsivity and severe mood swings.

### 5. How Quickly it Starts Working
*   **Onset of Action**: For acute mania, oral doses begin to reduce racing thoughts and severe agitation within **4 to 10 days**. 

### 6. Half-Life and Duration of Effect
*   The biological half-life is **9 to 16 hours**. Extended-release (CR/Chrono) forms slow absorption, allowing for once or twice-daily dosing to maintain a steady blood level.

## Section 2: Dosage, Forms & How to Take It

### 7. Standard Adult Dose & Frequency
*   **Acute Mania (Starting Dose)**: Often initiated rapidly at 25 mg/kg/day (e.g., 500mg twice daily).
*   **Maintenance Dose**: Generally ranges from 1000mg to 2500mg per day based on blood levels.

### 8. Available Forms
*   **Standard Tablets (Enteric Coated)**: 200mg, 500mg.
*   **Controlled Release (CR/Chrono)**: 200mg, 300mg, 500mg.
*   **Syrup/Oral Solution**: 200mg/5ml.
*   **Injection (IV)**: 400mg, 500mg.

### 9. How & When to Take It
*   **With Food**: **Always take with food** to prevent severe nausea.
*   **Do Not Crush**: CR, Chrono, or Enteric-Coated tablets must be swallowed whole. Crushing them destroys the slow-release mechanism, dumping the drug into your blood at once.

### 10. What to do if you miss a dose
*   Take it as soon as you remember. If it is almost time for your next dose, **skip the missed dose**. Never take a double dose.

### 11. Maximum Daily Dose
*   Rarely exceeds **60 mg/kg/day** (roughly 4000mg to 4500mg for a 75kg adult). Exceeding this risks severe liver failure and coma.

### 12. How to Store the Medicine
*   Store at room temperature (below 30°C). Keep away from direct sunlight, extreme heat, and moisture.
