# Olanzapine: Side Effects, Weight Gain, and Warnings (Part 2)

*Medical Disclaimer: This article is for informational and educational purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult your psychiatrist or physician before making any changes to your medication regimen.*

Olanzapine's ability to crush mania is unparalleled, but its efficacy comes with one of the most severe metabolic side effect profiles in modern psychiatry. Part 2 details the extreme weight gain, the risk of diabetes, and the regulatory warnings associated with it.

---

## Section 4: Side Effects & Safety Warnings

### 20. Common Side Effects (≥1 in 10 patients)
*   **Massive Weight Gain**: Olanzapine causes more rapid and severe weight gain than almost any other psychiatric drug. It is common to gain 10-20 kg in the first few months. It triggers hyperphagia (insatiable hunger for carbohydrates) and directly slows metabolism. **Weight loss** is almost impossible without extreme dietary intervention while on this drug.
*   **Profound Sedation**: Olanzapine is heavily sedating. Patients experience a "hangover" effect, making it extremely difficult to wake up the next morning.
*   **Dry Mouth & Constipation**: Caused by strong anticholinergic properties.
*   **Orthostatic Hypotension**: Severe dizziness or fainting when standing up quickly.

### 21. Serious/Rare Side Effects Needing Emergency Care
*   **Metabolic Syndrome & Diabetes**: A rapid, drug-induced spike in blood sugar, cholesterol, and triglycerides, which can precipitate full-blown Type-2 Diabetes and diabetic ketoacidosis.
*   **Tardive Dyskinesia (TD)**: Permanent, involuntary muscle movements (face, lips, jaw smacking). Risk increases with long-term use.
*   **Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS)**: A rare, potentially fatal reaction causing high fever, "lead-pipe" muscle rigidity, and autonomic instability.
*   **Extrapyramidal Symptoms (EPS)**: Parkinson-like symptoms (muscle stiffness, tremors, and severe inner restlessness called akathisia).

### 22. Black Box Warnings (FDA/CDSCO)
*   **Increased Mortality in Elderly Patients with Dementia**: Olanzapine carries a strict black box warning. Elderly patients with dementia-related psychosis treated with atypical antipsychotics are at an increased risk of sudden cardiovascular death or fatal pneumonia.

### 23. Signs of Toxicity or Overdose
*   **What to watch for**: Slurred speech, extreme sedation progressing to coma, agitation, aggression, tachycardia, and respiratory depression.
*   **What to do immediately**: Call emergency services. There is no specific antidote; treatment is supportive.

### 24. Withdrawal Symptoms & Risks
*   **Never Stop Abruptly**: Stopping Olanzapine suddenly will almost certainly trigger a "rebound psychosis" or severe manic relapse. It also causes intense physical withdrawal, including severe rebound insomnia, nausea, and extreme anxiety. It must be tapered over months.

### 25. Allergy and Hypersensitivity Reactions
*   **DRESS Syndrome**: Drug Reaction with Eosinophilia and Systemic Symptoms. A severe allergic reaction involving a skin rash, fever, and organ involvement. Requires immediate hospitalization.

### 26. Long-Term Side Effects
*   **Type-2 Diabetes**: The metabolic changes are profound and long-lasting, directly inducing insulin resistance.
*   **Hyperprolactinemia**: Elevated prolactin levels can cause breast enlargement in males (gynecomastia) and milk production or missed periods in females.
