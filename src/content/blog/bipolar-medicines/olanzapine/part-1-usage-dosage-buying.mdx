# Olanzapine: Uses, Dosage, and Buying Guide in India (Part 1)

*Medical Disclaimer: This article is for informational and educational purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult your psychiatrist or physician before making any changes to your medication regimen.*

Olanzapine is a heavy-duty atypical antipsychotic, primarily used to crush acute mania and psychosis in Bipolar Disorder. In this first part, we cover exactly what this medicine is, how it works to halt manic episodes, and how to buy it affordably in India.

---

## Section 1: What is this medicine & how it works

### 1. Drug Class & Category
*   **Primary Class**: Second-Generation (Atypical) Antipsychotic.
*   **Psychiatric Category**: Antimanic agent / Mood Stabilizer adjunct.

### 2. Mechanism of Action (Simple Language)
During a severe manic episode, the brain is flooded with dopamine, causing racing thoughts, delusions, and a total loss of the need for sleep. Olanzapine acts like a heavy blanket over the brain. It blocks the dopamine receptors, stopping hallucinations instantly. It also blocks histamine receptors, forcing the brain into a deep, heavy sleep to "reboot" the patient.
*   **Medical Detail**: It is a potent antagonist at Dopamine D1-D4 receptors, Serotonin 5-HT2A/C receptors, Histamine H1 receptors, and Alpha-1 adrenergic receptors. The D2 blockade manages psychosis, while the H1 blockade causes massive sedation and weight gain.

### 3. Approved Medical Uses (Indications)
*   **Bipolar I Disorder**: Acute treatment of manic or mixed episodes and maintenance treatment.
*   **Schizophrenia**: Acute and maintenance treatment.
*   **Treatment-Resistant Depression**: Used in combination with Fluoxetine.

### 4. Off-Label Uses
*   **Severe Insomnia**: Sometimes used in tiny doses purely as a sleep aid (though heavily frowned upon due to metabolic risks).
*   **Borderline Personality Disorder**: To manage severe anger and brief psychotic episodes.
*   **Chemotherapy-Induced Nausea**: Highly effective anti-emetic.

### 5. How Quickly it Starts Working
*   **Onset of Action**: For sedation, it works within **30 to 60 minutes**. For resolving acute manic psychosis, significant improvement is seen within **1 to 3 days**.

### 6. Half-Life and Duration of Effect
*   The biological half-life ranges from **21 to 54 hours**. This extremely long half-life contributes to the severe "next-day hangover" effect.

## Section 2: Dosage, Forms & How to Take It

### 7. Standard Adult Dose & Frequency
*   **Acute Mania (Starting Dose)**: Typically started at **10 mg to 15 mg once daily**.
*   **Maintenance Dose**: Generally ranges from 5 mg to 20 mg per day.

### 8. Available Forms
*   **Standard Oral Tablets**: 2.5mg, 5mg, 7.5mg, 10mg, 15mg, 20mg.
*   **Mouth-Dissolving (MD) Tablets (e.g., Olimelt)**: Dissolve instantly on the tongue. Highly useful for manic patients who try to hide pills.
*   **Intramuscular (IM) Injection**: Short-acting injection for psychiatric emergencies.

### 9. How & When to Take It
*   **Time of Day**: Because it is profoundly sedating, the entire dose should be taken **at bedtime**. Taking it in the morning will render the patient unable to function.
*   **MD Tablets**: Handle with dry hands, place on the tongue, and swallow with saliva.

### 10. What to do if you miss a dose
*   Take it as soon as you remember. If it is almost time for your next dose, skip it. Never double up.

### 11. Maximum Daily Dose
*   The maximum recommended daily dose is **20 mg**. Higher doses provide zero additional clinical benefit but exponentially increase the risk of Tardive Dyskinesia and diabetes.

### 12. How to Store the Medicine
*   Store at room temperature (below 30°C). Protect MD tablets heavily from moisture.
