## 1. LenDenClub

### Overview
LenDenClub is one of India's largest P2P platforms by lender base. It uses AI/ML algorithms for credit scoring and offers micro-diversification down to ₹250 per borrower.

### Strengths
*   **Micro-diversification**: ₹250 minimum per loan allows spreading across 100+ borrowers easily.
*   **Low NPA**: Reported NPA of ~3.62% (as of late 2025) is among the lowest in the industry.
*   **Profitability**: Turned profitable in FY25 after restructuring.
*   **Technology**: Advanced credit scoring using AI/ML, bureau data, and alternative data points.

### Weaknesses
*   **Fee transparency**: Some investors have raised concerns about fee structures impacting net returns.
*   **Recovery opacity**: Limited visibility into the recovery process for defaulted loans.
*   **Liquidity**: Post-August 2024, no early exit feature. Funds locked until loan maturity.

---

## 2. Lendbox

### Overview
Lendbox is an RBI-registered platform that offers various risk-return categories. It provides detailed portfolio analytics and has been transparent about its NPA data.

### Strengths
*   **Transparency**: Publicly discloses Gross NPA data (reported at ~9.75%).
*   **Risk Categories**: Offers multiple risk buckets allowing investors to choose their risk appetite.
*   **Detailed Analytics**: Portfolio-level performance dashboards for investors.

### Weaknesses
*   **Higher NPA**: The ~9.75% Gross NPA is the highest among major platforms, meaning roughly 1 in 10 loans has defaulted.
*   **Higher Minimum**: ₹1,000 minimum per loan limits micro-diversification for small investors.
*   **Net Returns Impact**: After accounting for defaults and fees, actual net returns may be significantly lower than advertised.

---

## 3. Faircent

### Overview
India's first RBI-registered P2P lending platform (founded 2014). It positions itself as a marketplace with detailed credit grading.

### Strengths
*   **Regulatory Pioneer**: First mover in the regulated P2P space.
*   **Credit Grading System**: Uses an A-E grading system for borrowers with corresponding interest rates.
*   **Performance Decks**: Publishes periodic performance data for investor reference.

### Weaknesses
*   **Higher Entry Barrier**: ₹10,000 minimum investment limits accessibility.
*   **"Best Effort" Recovery**: Explicitly states that repayments are on a "best effort" basis — not guaranteed.
*   **Platform Age**: Older codebase and interface compared to newer competitors.

---

## 4. LiquiLoans (NDX P2P)

### Overview
Positions itself as a "prime quality" borrower-focused platform, targeting lower-risk segments.

### Strengths
*   **Borrower Quality**: Claims to focus on prime and near-prime borrowers, potentially resulting in lower default rates.
*   **RBI Licensed**: Fully compliant NBFC-P2P.

### Weaknesses
*   **Limited Public Data**: Less publicly available NPA and performance data compared to LenDenClub and Lendbox.
*   **Smaller Scale**: Smaller lender and borrower base compared to industry leaders.

---

## 5. 12% Club / BharatPe Invest

### Overview
Originally launched by BharatPe as "12% Club," it acted as a digital intermediary connecting users to partner NBFC-P2P platforms. Has been rebranded post-2024 regulations.

### Critical Warning
*   **Not a Direct P2P Platform**: It is an intermediary that routes your money to partner NBFCs. Understand which underlying platform is actually managing your loans.
*   **Post-Regulation Changes**: The "12% guaranteed" branding was removed following the August 2024 RBI crackdown. The actual returns are now market-dependent and subject to borrower defaults.
